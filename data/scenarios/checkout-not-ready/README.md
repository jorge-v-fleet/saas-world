# Scenario: checkout-not-ready

Archetype: **hidden-critical-blocker**. Your first week. Self-serve checkout is reported on track to launch Friday. It isn't.

## The situation
- The launch is your responsibility (you are PM A).
- The payment integration is being done by Priya — a backend engineer on another PM's team.
- Priya knows the payment provider won't be certified until the following week, so live payments would fail. She hasn't said so.

## Who knows what
- **Priya** (backend, another team) — holds the real blocker; reveals it only if asked directly about payment/checkout readiness.
- **Sam** (your frontend engineer) — knows his UI is stuck waiting on Priya, and points you to her, but doesn't know why.
- **Rohit** (CTO, your manager) — committed Friday to the CEO, pushes for a confident status, and does not know about the blocker.

## What good looks like
- Surface the certification blocker (by talking to Priya, possibly via Sam).
- Move the launch, or record a go/no-go decision.
- Tell Rohit the real reason.
- If you write the decision down: name the blocker, a new date, and an owner.

## What won't earn credit
- Sending lots of messages, or marking tasks "in progress" — none of that clears the blocker or counts toward the score.

## Timing
- A five-day work week; launch is end of day 5.
- The certification clears only *after* the launch — that gap is the whole problem.
- Scripted pressure: the CTO checks in on day 2, pushes hard on day 4. Everything else (coworker replies, their own moves) happens on realistic delays.
- All times are relative to the week's start (day offsets) — no fixed calendar dates.
